# Signal to Noise - Quick Play Guide v2

## 5-Minute Setup
1. Put 6 conspiracy cards on table
2. Flip a coin for each: heads = REAL, tails = FAKE (keep secret)
3. Everyone gets 3 evidence cards, credibility = 5, audience = 0
4. Ready to play!

---

## Every Round (3 Steps)

### 1. INVESTIGATE
- Draw 2 evidence cards (max 5 in hand)
- Put cards face-down under conspiracies you want to claim
- Locked in once placed

### 2. BROADCAST (take turns)
Pick one:
- **Broadcast:** Say "Conspiracy X is REAL/FAKE" (goes in public queue)
- **Pass:** Don't broadcast, draw 1 extra card

Everyone can see the queue before deciding.

### 3. RESOLVE
- Check each conspiracy: did 3+ people claim the same thing?
- **YES = CONSENSUS:** Reveal truth, score points
  - Right: gain (evidence cards × conspiracy tier) audience
  - Wrong: lose 3 credibility
- **NO:** Nothing happens, move on

Replace revealed conspiracies, start new round.

---

## Win Condition
**Most audience when game ends**

Game ends: 12 conspiracies revealed OR 6 rounds OR anyone hits 60 audience

---

## Core Strategy

**The Queue Is Everything:**
- You see what others claimed before you decide
- 2 people already said "REAL"? Join as 3rd = consensus triggers
- 2 people said "FAKE"? Disagree and pass, or agree and score

**Build Strong Cases:**
- 3-4 evidence on one conspiracy > 1 evidence on four conspiracies
- More evidence = more points when right

**When to Pass:**
- Queue looks wrong (avoid losing credibility)
- Need more evidence (draw cards for next round)
- Want to see what happens (gather info)

## Example Turn

**Board has:** "Moon Landing" (2★), "Chemtrails" (1★)

**Turn order:**
1. Alice: "Moon Landing = FAKE" (2 evidence)
2. Bob: "Moon Landing = FAKE" (3 evidence)
3. Carol sees both → "Moon Landing = FAKE" (2 evidence) ← triggers consensus!

**Resolution:**
- Reveal Moon Landing truth: FAKE ✓
- Alice: 2 evidence × 2★ = 4 audience
- Bob: 3 evidence × 2★ = 6 audience
- Carol: 2 evidence × 2★ = 4 audience

All three were right, all scored.

## Advanced Rules (Optional)

### Counter-Broadcast
- Take opposite position from someone in queue
- Conspiracy resolves immediately as confrontation
- Winner gets double points + steals 5 audience from loser
- Loser loses 5 credibility

**When to use:** You're confident they're wrong, you have strong evidence.

### Credibility Effects
- High credibility (7-10): Only need 1 more person for consensus (instead of 2)
- Low credibility (0-3): Need 4 people for consensus (instead of 3)
- Makes reputation matter

### Exposé Action
- Spend 10 audience to reveal any conspiracy immediately
- Once per game
- Use to confirm suspicions or trap opponents

---

## Quick Reference

**Consensus trigger:** 3+ broadcasts on same conspiracy, same position  
**Scoring:** Evidence cards × Conspiracy tier  
**Wrong penalty:** -3 credibility  
**Hand limit:** 5 evidence cards  
**Pass bonus:** +1 evidence card

---

That's it! Simple rules, deep decisions. The game is about reading your opponents and timing your claims.

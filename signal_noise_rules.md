# Signal to Noise - Rules Document v2

## Game Overview

**Players:** 2-4  
**Time:** 30-45 minutes  
**Goal:** Build the largest podcast audience by correctly exposing conspiracy theories

You are competing podcasters investigating conspiracy theories. Some conspiracies are real, some are fake. You don't know which is which. Build your case, make your claim, and hope you're right.

---

## Components

### Conspiracy Board
- 6 active conspiracy theory cards displayed publicly
- Each has a hidden TRUTH VALUE (Real or Fake)
- Each has a TIER (1★, 2★, or 3★) showing points if you're correct

### Evidence Deck
- 60 evidence cards
- Each card shows which conspiracies it could support (icons)
- Evidence is worth 1 point each when revealed

### Player Boards
Each player has:
- **Credibility Track** (0-10, starts at 5) - used only for tiebreaker
- **Audience Counter** (victory points)
- **Evidence Hand** (max 5 cards)
- **Investigation Area** (face-down evidence assigned to conspiracies)

### Broadcast Queue
- Central timeline showing all broadcasts this round
- Shows: Player, Conspiracy, Position (Real/Fake), Evidence count

---

## Setup

1. Place 6 conspiracy cards on the board
2. Secretly assign each a truth token (Real or Fake) - flip a coin for each
3. Shuffle evidence deck, deal 3 cards to each player
4. Set all players' credibility to 5, audience to 0
5. Randomly determine first player

---

## How to Play

Each round has 3 simple phases:

### Phase 1: INVESTIGATE

All players simultaneously:
1. Draw 2 evidence cards (hand limit: 5 cards)
2. Assign evidence cards face-down to conspiracies on your board
3. Once assigned, cards are locked in (can't move them)

*You're building your case. More evidence = more points if you're right.*

### Phase 2: BROADCAST

In turn order, each player chooses ONE action:

**Option A: Broadcast Episode**
- Pick a conspiracy you have evidence for
- Declare: "This conspiracy is REAL" or "This conspiracy is FAKE"
- Your broadcast goes in the public QUEUE
- Everyone can see: which conspiracy, your position, how many evidence cards you have

**Option B: Pass**
- Don't broadcast this round
- Your PASS is visible to everyone
- Draw 1 additional evidence card

*Key rule: You can see all previous broadcasts before you decide what to do.*

### Phase 3: RESOLVE

Go through the queue in broadcast order:

**For each broadcast:**
1. Reveal your evidence cards
2. Count your evidence: 1 point per card

**Check for CONSENSUS:**
- If 3 or more broadcasts claimed the same position on the same conspiracy → CONSENSUS!
- Immediately reveal that conspiracy's truth value
- **Correct broadcasts:** Gain audience = (evidence count × conspiracy tier)
- **Wrong broadcasts:** Lose 3 credibility, no audience gained

**If no consensus:**
- Broadcast is discarded (no scoring)
- Conspiracy stays hidden

**Phase 4: CLEANUP**
1. Replace any revealed conspiracies with new ones
2. Clear the queue
3. Pass first player marker
4. Start next round

---

## Winning the Game

Game ends when:
- 12 conspiracies have been revealed OR
- 6 rounds have been played OR
- Any player reaches 60 audience

**Winner:** Highest audience  
**Tiebreaker:** Highest credibility

---

## Key Strategic Concepts

### Reading the Queue

The queue is public information. You see what others claimed BEFORE you decide.

**Example:**
- Alice broadcasts first: "Moon landing is FAKE" (3 evidence)
- Bob broadcasts second: "Moon landing is FAKE" (2 evidence)
- You go third: Do you join them as the 3rd person (triggering consensus)? Or pass?

If you join and they're right: all three of you score.  
If you join and they're wrong: all three of you lose credibility.  
If you pass: you gain info but score nothing this round.

### Consensus Triggers Everything

Only broadcasts that hit consensus (3+ players) get resolved. This means:
- Being first or second to broadcast is risky (might not reach consensus)
- Being third is powerful (you trigger the resolution)
- Being fourth is wasteful (consensus already happened)

### Evidence as Commitment

You assign evidence BEFORE seeing the queue. This creates tension:
- Put all evidence on one conspiracy? (Strong claim, but one bet)
- Spread evidence across multiple? (Flexibility, but weaker claims)

### Pass Signals

When you pass, others can infer:
- You don't have strong evidence
- You think someone in the queue is right
- You're waiting for more information

Passing too much means you never score. Broadcasting blindly means you'll be wrong often.

## Example Round

**Conspiracies on board:**
- "Moon Landing Fake" (2★)
- "Chemtrails" (1★)  
- "Mayor Embezzlement" (3★)

**Phase 1: Investigate**
- Alice draws 2 cards, assigns 2 to Moon Landing, 1 to Mayor
- Bob draws 2 cards, assigns 3 to Moon Landing
- Carol draws 2 cards, assigns 2 to Chemtrails

**Phase 2: Broadcast**
1. Alice goes first: "Moon Landing is FAKE" (shows 2 evidence in queue)
2. Bob sees Alice's claim: "Moon Landing is FAKE" (shows 3 evidence in queue)
3. Carol sees both: Passes (draws 1 card)

**Phase 3: Resolve**
- Only 2 broadcasts on Moon Landing = no consensus
- Both broadcasts are discarded, no scoring
- Carol's pass means she scored nothing but has more evidence for next round

**Next round:**
- Alice and Bob might be frustrated (wasted evidence)
- Carol has information: they both think Moon Landing is fake
- She can join them next time if she agrees, or stay silent if she disagrees

---

## Strategy Tips

**Beginner Strategy: Join the Crowd**
- Wait to see 2 people agree
- If you agree with them, broadcast as the 3rd
- Safe, reliable, small gains

**Intermediate Strategy: Lead the Pack**  
- Broadcast early with strong evidence (4+ cards)
- Hope others follow you
- Higher risk (might not reach consensus), higher reward (more evidence = more points)

**Advanced Strategy: Read the Passes**
- If someone passes after seeing 2 broadcasts, they probably disagree
- If multiple people pass, the broadcasts are probably wrong
- Use this info to avoid bad consensus

---

## Common Mistakes

❌ Broadcasting every round (wastes weak evidence)  
❌ Ignoring the queue (you MUST react to what others do)  
❌ Never passing (sometimes you need more info)  
❌ Spreading evidence too thin (1 card per conspiracy is weak)

✓ Build strong cases (3-4 evidence per conspiracy)  
✓ Watch for 2-broadcast setups (be the 3rd)  
✓ Pass when the queue looks wrong  
✓ Target higher-tier conspiracies (better multipliers)

# ADVANCED RULES MODULE

*Add these rules after players understand the base game (3-5 plays)*

## Advanced Action: Counter-Broadcast

**During Broadcast phase, new option:**

**Counter-Broadcast:**
- Choose an existing broadcast from the queue
- Take the OPPOSITE position on that conspiracy
- You're now in direct conflict with that player

**Resolution changes:**
- If you counter-broadcast someone, consensus is IMPOSSIBLE for that conspiracy this round
- Instead, that conspiracy resolves immediately as a CONFRONTATION
- Reveal the truth value right away
- **Winner:** Gains (evidence × tier × 2) audience, plus steals 5 audience from loser
- **Loser:** Loses 5 credibility, gives 5 audience to winner

**Why this is advanced:**
- Adds direct conflict (more confrontational)
- Higher stakes (double points, stealing audience)
- Requires reading opponents (are they bluffing or confident?)
- Changes the game from cooperative consensus to adversarial competition

**Strategic implications:**
- Counter-broadcasting is high risk, high reward
- You're betting you're right AND they're wrong
- Best used when you have strong evidence and they look weak
- Can be used to punish weak broadcasts early in queue

## Advanced Mechanic: Credibility Effects

**Instead of credibility being just a tiebreaker, it now affects gameplay:**

**High Credibility (7-10):**
- When you broadcast, others trust you
- If 2 players at 7+ credibility agree, only need 1 more for consensus (instead of 3 total)
- But if you're wrong, you lose 5 credibility instead of 3

**Low Credibility (0-3):**
- When you broadcast, others doubt you
- Need 4 total broadcasts (instead of 3) to form consensus if you're involved
- But if you're wrong, you only lose 1 credibility

**Why this is advanced:**
- Adds reputation management layer
- High-credibility players become "opinion leaders"
- Low-credibility players can make wild swings with less penalty
- Creates asymmetric player dynamics

## Advanced Action: Exposé

**Once per game, during Broadcast phase:**

Instead of broadcasting, spend 10 audience to FORCE immediate revelation of any conspiracy.

**Why this is advanced:**
- Spending audience to gain information is a tough decision
- Can be used to confirm your suspicions before committing
- Can be used to screw opponents who broadcast wrong positions
- Adds timing/resource management layer

## Advanced Mechanic: Special Evidence Cards

**Add 10 special evidence cards to the deck:**

**"Smoking Gun" (rare)**
- Worth 3 evidence points instead of 1
- Only supports one specific conspiracy
- If you broadcast with a Smoking Gun and you're right, gain +5 bonus audience

**"Red Herring" (trap)**
- Worth 1 evidence point
- Supports all conspiracies
- But if you're wrong, you lose 5 credibility instead of 3 (it looks like you fell for fake evidence)

**Why this is advanced:**
- Adds variance and card evaluation
- Smoking Guns create high-stakes moments
- Red Herrings add risk to using generic evidence
- More decisions about which evidence to use

---

## Mixing Advanced Modules

You can add advanced rules incrementally:

**Level 1 (Base Game):** Consensus only, simple scoring  
**Level 2:** Add Counter-Broadcasts  
**Level 3:** Add Credibility Effects  
**Level 4:** Add Exposé action  
**Level 5 (Full Game):** Add Special Evidence Cards

Each level adds complexity but also strategic depth. Most players will be happy at Level 2 or 3.

---

## Design Philosophy

**Base game:** Accessible, teaches in 5 minutes, focuses on the core insight (common knowledge from seeing the queue).

**Advanced rules:** For experienced players who want more interaction, more conflict, more strategic options.

The base game is a complete, satisfying experience. The advanced rules are for players who want to go deeper, not bandaids for a broken core design.

SIGNAL TO NOISE Quick Reference Card

GAME SETUP

  • 3-6 players
  • 6 rounds, 5 active conspiracies per round
  • Starting hand: 5 evidence cards
  • Draw 2 per round (max hand: 7)
  • Consensus threshold: ceil(playerCount / 2)
  • Starting credibility: 5 (range 0-10)

TURN ORDER

Each round: lowest score goes first.
Ties broken by lowest credibility.

PHASE 1: COMMIT

Secretly assign evidence cards from your hand to active conspiracies. Each card placed face-down on one conspiracy. You may assign multiple cards or none.

  • Generic (○) cards go on ANY conspiracy
  • Specific (◆) cards ONLY on their named conspiracy

PHASE 2: BROADCAST

In turn order, each player either:

  • Declares REAL or FAKE on one active conspiracy
  • PASS — skip, score 0 for all conspiracies

Sequential: you see previous players' declarations before choosing.

PHASE 3: RESOLVE

For each conspiracy with broadcasts:

  1. Count REAL vs FAKE declarations
  2. If majority ≥ threshold → consensus reached
  3. Reveal committed evidence
  4. Score points (see Scoring)
  5. Adjust credibility

CREDIBILITY

  • On majority side: +1 credibility
  • On minority side: -1 credibility
  • Range: 0 to 10 (clamped)
  • Used as tiebreaker only

EVIDENCE CARDS

  • Generic (○) — 12 cards, support ALL conspiracies
  • Specific (◆) — 36 cards (3 per conspiracy), +1 bonus point

WINNING

After 6 rounds, highest total score wins.
Tiebreaker: highest credibility.

BETWEEN ROUNDS

  • Discard evidence from resolved conspiracies
  • Replace resolved conspiracies to refill to 5
  • Draw 2 evidence cards (max hand: 7)
  • Recalculate turn order (lowest score first)

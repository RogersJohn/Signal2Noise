SIGNAL TO NOISE

VERSION 2

A game of conspiracy, bluffing, and manufactured consensus

3-6 Players • 30-45 Minutes • Ages 14+

OVERVIEW

You are a broadcaster in the post-truth media landscape. Conspiracies are everywhere — some might even be real. Your job isn't to find the truth. It's to be on the winning side.

Each round, conspiracies hit the airwaves. You'll secretly commit evidence, then publicly declare each conspiracy REAL or FAKE. Land on the majority side to score points. Back your position with evidence to score even more. Read the room, bluff strategically, and manufacture consensus.

The player with the most points after 6 rounds wins.

COMPONENTS

12 Conspiracy Cards
12 Generic Evidence Cards (support all conspiracies)
36 Specific Evidence Cards (3 per conspiracy)
1 Quick Reference Card
— Score tracking (pen & paper or app)

KEY CONCEPTS

Consensus — When enough broadcasters agree (REAL or FAKE), a conspiracy reaches consensus. The threshold is ceil(playerCount / 2).

Evidence — Cards you secretly commit to conspiracies during the Commit phase. Evidence on the majority side earns bonus points. Specific evidence (marked ◆) earns an extra bonus.

Credibility — Starts at 5 (range 0–10). Goes up when you're on the majority, down when you're in the minority. Used only as a tiebreaker.

SETUP

  1. Shuffle all 12 Conspiracy Cards. Deal 5 face-up to form the active conspiracies. Set the rest aside as a conspiracy draw pile.
  2. Shuffle all 48 Evidence Cards together (generic + specific). Deal 5 to each player as their starting hand. Place the rest face-down as the evidence draw pile.
  3. Each player starts with 0 points and 5 credibility.
  4. Determine first-round turn order randomly.

ROUND STRUCTURE

The game lasts 6 rounds. Each round has three phases:

PHASE 1: COMMIT

All players act simultaneously. Look at your hand of evidence cards and the 5 active conspiracies. Secretly assign any number of evidence cards from your hand face-down to conspiracies.

  • Each evidence card goes to exactly one conspiracy
  • Generic evidence (○) can go on ANY conspiracy
  • Specific evidence (◆) can ONLY go on its named conspiracy
  • You may assign zero cards (keep your hand for future rounds)
  • Committed cards are placed face-down — other players don't see them yet

Once all players are done committing, move to the Broadcast phase.

PHASE 2: BROADCAST

Players take turns in order (lowest score first; ties broken by lowest credibility). On your turn, you must either:

  • Declare REAL or FAKE on one active conspiracy, OR
  • PASS — skip broadcasting entirely this round (score 0 for everything)

Declarations are public. You can see what previous players declared before making your choice. This is where the bluffing and reading happens.

You broadcast on exactly one conspiracy per round (or pass).

PHASE 3: RESOLVE

After all players have broadcast (or passed), resolve each conspiracy that received at least one broadcast:

  1. Count votes: Tally REAL vs FAKE declarations
  2. Check consensus: Does the majority reach the threshold (ceil(playerCount / 2))?
  3. Reveal evidence: Flip all committed evidence cards face-up
  4. Score points: Award points per the scoring table (see page 3)
  5. Adjust credibility: +1 for majority side, -1 for minority side

Scoring Example

4 players. Consensus threshold = ceil(4/2) = 2. Three players broadcast on "Chemtrails": Alice says REAL with specific evidence, Bob says REAL with generic evidence, Carol says FAKE with no evidence. Dave passed.

REAL wins (2 vs 1, meets threshold of 2).

  • Alice: 3 (evidence) + 1 (specific) + 1 (first mover) + 0 (no extra beyond threshold) = 5 pts
  • Bob: 3 (evidence) + 0 (generic) + 0 (not first) + 0 = 3 pts
  • Carol: minority side = 0 pts, credibility -1
  • Dave: passed = 0 pts

Alice and Bob each get +1 credibility.

CREDIBILITY

  • +1 when you're on the majority side of a consensus
  • -1 when you're on the minority side
  • Clamped to range 0–10
  • Credibility is used only as a tiebreaker for score ties and turn order

BETWEEN ROUNDS

  1. Discard all evidence from resolved conspiracies to the discard pile
  2. Remove resolved conspiracies; deal new ones from the conspiracy deck to refill to 5 active
  3. Each player draws 2 evidence cards (max hand size: 7; discard excess to your choice)
  4. Recalculate turn order: lowest score first, ties broken by lowest credibility

GAME END

The game ends after 6 rounds. The player with the highest total score wins. In case of a tie, the player with the highest credibility wins. If still tied, players share the victory.

STRATEGY TIPS

Read the room. Broadcasting is sequential — watch what others declare before committing to a position. Going first is risky but earns the first mover bonus.

Evidence is leverage. Committing evidence shows you have skin in the game. But you're also giving up cards from your hand for future rounds.

The bandwagon is real. You score 2 points just for being on the winning side, even without evidence. Sometimes going with the crowd is the smart play.

Specific evidence is gold. The +1 bonus for specific evidence adds up fast. Save specific cards for when you can guarantee the majority.

Passing is valid. If the round looks unfavorable, passing preserves your hand and avoids credibility loss. Zero points beats a credibility hit.

Credibility matters late. In tight games, credibility is the tiebreaker. Don't tank it early by taking losing positions.

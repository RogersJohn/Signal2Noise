📡 SIGNAL TO NOISE

A Social Deduction Card Game for 3-5 Players

What Is This Game?

You are conspiracy investigators competing to build consensus. The truth doesn't matter — only what the group agrees on. Commit secret evidence, broadcast your position, and score points when the majority sides with you.

Components

  • 12 Conspiracy cards (5 active at a time)
  • 48 Evidence cards (12 generic + 36 specific)
  • Pen and paper for tracking scores and credibility

Setup

  1. Shuffle conspiracies. Place 5 face-up in the center. Stack the rest as a draw pile.
  2. Shuffle evidence cards. Deal 5 to each player.
  3. Each player starts at 0 points, 5 credibility.
  4. Determine first player randomly. After round 1, lowest score goes first.

How to Play — 6 Rounds

Phase 1: COMMIT

In turn order, each player assigns evidence cards from their hand to active conspiracies. Cards are placed face-down next to the conspiracy. Other players see how many cards you assigned, but not which cards.

  • Generic cards (📋) support any conspiracy.
  • Specific cards (🎯) only support the conspiracy they name.
  • You may assign 0 or more cards. Say "done" when finished.

Phase 2: BROADCAST

Players act one at a time in turn order. Each broadcast is immediately visible to everyone. This is the core of the game — later players have more information.

On your turn, either:

  • Broadcast: Pick one active conspiracy and declare it REAL or FAKE.
  • Pass: Do nothing this round.

You may only act once per round. No changing your mind.

Phase 3: RESOLVE

For each conspiracy that received broadcasts:

  1. Count REAL vs FAKE broadcasts.
  2. Check if either side meets the consensus threshold (see below).
  3. If consensus: score points, adjust credibility, remove and replace the conspiracy.
  4. If no consensus: nobody scores. The conspiracy stays.

After resolving, each player draws 2 cards (max hand size: 7).

Consensus Threshold

PlayersThreshold
3–42 must agree
53 must agree

Credibility

Majority: +1 | Minority: -1 | Range: 0–10 | Starts at 5

Credibility is ONLY a tiebreaker. It does not affect scoring.

Winning

After 6 rounds, highest score wins. Ties broken by credibility.

Strategy Tips

  • The first mover bonus rewards bold broadcasts, but you act with less information.
  • Watch how many cards opponents commit — it reveals their plans.
  • Bandwagoning (2 pts, no evidence needed) is efficient but limited.
  • Specific evidence gives a bonus — focus your cards on one conspiracy.
  • Building large consensus benefits everyone on the majority side.

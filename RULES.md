# Signal to Noise — Rules

## Overview & Setup

**Signal to Noise** is a social deduction card game for 3–5 players about conspiracy theories, evidence, and consensus. Players investigate active conspiracies by committing evidence and broadcasting their position. The truth doesn't matter — only what the group *agrees* on.

### Components
- 12 Conspiracy cards (5 active at a time)
- 48 Evidence cards (12 generic, 36 specific)
- Score tracker (pen & paper)

### Setup
1. Shuffle the Conspiracy deck. Place 5 face-up in the center — these are the **active conspiracies**. Set the rest aside as a draw pile.
2. Shuffle the Evidence deck. Deal **5 cards** to each player.
3. Each player starts with **5 credibility** and **0 points**.
4. The player with the lowest score goes first each round. On the first round, choose randomly.

---

## How to Play

The game lasts **6 rounds**. Each round has three phases:

### Phase 1: COMMIT

In turn order, each player secretly assigns evidence cards from their hand to active conspiracies. Other players can see **how many** cards you assigned to each conspiracy, but **not which cards**.

- You may assign 0 or more cards.
- Generic cards (📋) can support any conspiracy.
- Specific cards (🎯) can only support the conspiracy they name.
- Cards assigned are removed from your hand.

When finished, declare "done." Once all players are done, move to Broadcast.

### Phase 2: BROADCAST

Players broadcast **one at a time, in turn order**. Each broadcast is immediately visible to everyone — later players can react to earlier broadcasts.

On your turn, choose one:
- **Broadcast**: Pick an active conspiracy and declare it **REAL** or **FAKE**.
- **Pass**: Do nothing.

You may only broadcast once per round.

### Phase 3: RESOLVE

For each conspiracy that received broadcasts, check for **consensus**.

**Consensus threshold**: majority needed = ⌈players ÷ 2⌉
- 3–4 players: 2 needed
- 5 players: 3 needed

If enough players chose the same position (REAL or FAKE), consensus is reached. That conspiracy is resolved and replaced from the deck. Score points (see below). If no consensus, nobody scores on that conspiracy.

After resolving, each player **draws 2 cards** (max hand size: 7).

---

## Scoring

| Situation | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Majority side, with evidence committed | **3** |
| …and evidence is specific to this conspiracy | **+1** |
| Majority side, no evidence (bandwagon) | **2** |
| First to broadcast on a conspiracy that reaches consensus | **+1** |
| Each additional player on majority beyond threshold | **+1** |
| Minority side | **0** |
| No consensus reached | **0** |
| Pass | **0** |

### Credibility
- Majority side: **+1 credibility**
- Minority side: **−1 credibility**
- Range: 0–10. Starts at 5.
- Credibility is **only a tiebreaker** at end of game. It does not multiply scoring.

### Winning
After 6 rounds, the player with the **highest score** wins. Tie: highest credibility wins.

---

## Strategy Tips

- **Going first is risky but rewarding.** The first mover bonus is valuable, but you broadcast without information.
- **Watch what others commit.** The number of cards assigned (even hidden) reveals intent.
- **Bandwagoning is viable but limited.** 2 points with no evidence investment — efficient, but you need others to commit evidence.
- **Specific evidence is powerful.** The +1 specific bonus plus the base 3 makes focused play strong.
- **Consensus size matters.** Building a large consensus benefits everyone on the majority side. Sometimes it's better to join than to oppose.
